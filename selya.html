To most, Selya is an anomaly. Even to members of her own race, the woman is an unwelcome curiosity - feral, savage, and almost ethereal. Those who would describe her do so with a mixture of curiosity and distrust. Not a Dunmer noble of the Great Houses, not an Ashlander, or even a slave or servant. Most in Balmorra remember her as a dirty naked child, with a wildness in her eyes that would never be pulled from her.

Personality

Selya is a fairly quiet woman, preferring to listen rather than speak up, though when she does speak, her words are meaningful. This is not a woman who readily engages in pointless chatter; if one should speak, let the words matter. Of course, this tends to slightly change when around people she trusts (even though those are few and far between). She is blunt and somewhat cynical, often giving way to practicality over empathy. However, it has been proven in more than a few cases that empathy, while a somewhat stunted, hesitant entity in Selya's mind, is nonetheless one of her driving forces. Her connection to nature could not exist without it, even if it does not always show itself around mer and men.

Still, the woman holds no prejudices, except to those that would do harm. She remains poised and seemingly unfazed my most things; at least, if something does faze her, you will likely never see her reaction, or you will be floored by it. Outbursts of emotion are not her preferred way of dealing with most situations; if she is ever angered or disappointed, she will show it through actions. Talking through strong emotions is nigh impossible for Selya, and she prefers to distract herself rather than deal with them directly unless necessary.

From spending a good portion of her childhood in the wilderness, only to be thrust into a 'civilized' lifestyle prior to adolesence, one might believe Selya's mind would have torn in two. Instead, it appears to have embraced both cultures; while she rejects the society expected of her, the woman she is today is far from the feral youth she'd been before. Her animalistic cunning and intelligence has only been enhanced by growing up with a Dunmer family, and she will freely admit that it was likely the best possible outcome. She is extremely introspective, possibly to a dangerous extent - talking about her problems and concerns to others who are not animals isn't going to happen, and discussing her plans and motivations may happen with some prodding. If her head isn't in her poisons and potions, it's in her collection of books and scrolls.

She is quite skilled with a bow and staff, and is a great deal less frail than she appears to be. Still, Selya is often found on the outskirts of battle, placing traps and wards rather than staying in the thick of battle. While not directly aligned with the Wyresses, as she would say, Selya shares the connection and the affinity for nature magic - the goals are the same, but the distrust is still present. It does not bother her in the least, and she's happy enough to assist from afar. If there were anything to make the usually unruffled woman start to break down, it would be a shaky connection to the Earth, or something tainting or dampening it.
